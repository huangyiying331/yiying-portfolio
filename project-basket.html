Market Basket Analysis & Association Rules

Transaction logs · Apriori · Lift-based ranking

This project implements market basket analysis to uncover co-purchase patterns and actionable cross-sell rules. The pipeline covers robust cleaning/normalization, basketization, frequent itemsets via Apriori, and association rules filtered by lift and confidence. Visualizations summarize the most purchased items, item-pair co-occurrence, and the structure of high-lift rules.

Problem & Data

  • Input: transaction-level item logs (transaction ID, item name, optional time segments).
  • Goal: identify product affinities to guide bundling, shelf placement, and promotions.
  • Normalization: item strings are cleaned (trim/title-case); invalid/empty items removed.

Basketization & Dimensionality Control

Transactions are converted into a binary basket matrix. Rare items are dropped using a global item support threshold (MIN_ITEM_GLOBAL_SUPPORT = 0.005) to reduce sparsity and speed up mining.

Frequent Itemsets & Rules

Frequent itemsets are mined with Apriori (min_support = 0.01). Association rules are then generated and filtered to keep lift > 1 and confidence ≥ 0.30, sorted by lift → confidence → support. The scatter below shows support vs. confidence (bubble size proportional to lift), and the network reveals high-lift antecedent→consequent links.

Segmented Rules (Optional)

If time segments exist (period_day, weekday_weekend), the pipeline mines separate rules per segment and exports them. This highlights context-specific affinities (e.g., weekend brunch items vs. weekday coffee pairings).

Business Implications

  • Cross-sell & bundles: promote high-lift pairs/sets together to increase basket size.
  • Merchandising: co-located shelf placement for frequent co-occurrences.
  • Promotions: target top antecedents with offers nudging the consequent purchase.
  • Time-aware strategy: segment-specific rules can inform day-part menus and staffing.

Artifacts & Reproducibility

  • Tables: frequent_itemsets.csv, association_rules.csv
  • Figures: top_items.png, item_pair_heatmap.png, rules_scatter.png, rules_network.png
  • Optional segments: association_rules_period_day=*.csv, association_rules_weekday_weekend=*.csv
  • Key params: MIN_ITEM_GLOBAL_SUPPORT=0.005, MIN_SUPPORT=0.01, MIN_CONF=0.30

Tech Stack: Python · pandas · numpy · mlxtend (apriori, association_rules) · networkx · matplotlib
