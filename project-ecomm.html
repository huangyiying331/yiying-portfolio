E-commerce Customer Analytics

Customer behavior dataset · n = 350

This project builds an end-to-end analytics pipeline in R to uncover relationships between customer traits, spending behavior, and satisfaction. The workflow covers robust data preparation, unsupervised segmentation, explanatory modeling, and diagnostic validation to support data-driven segmentation and targeted operations.

Data Preparation

The raw dataset was cleaned, encoded, and standardized to form a tidy analysis table with no missing values. This ensures model stability and comparability across features of different scales.

Unsupervised Segmentation

Customers were clustered with K-Means and visualized via PCA. The final solution yields three interpretable segments: Dormant, Mid-value, and High-value Active, differing in age, spending capacity, and activity.

Regression Models

Two linear models were built:

  • Model A (Total Spend): Purchase quantity, discount usage, and city are the primary drivers of spend. The model attains a high fit (R² = 0.9984).
  • Model B (Average Rating): Age, discount usage, and location significantly affect ratings, while spend and purchase frequency have smaller effects, suggesting satisfaction relates more to service and psychological factors. Fit is strong (R² = 0.9631).

Diagnostics

Residual and Q-Q diagnostics indicate both models broadly satisfy linear regression assumptions, supporting explanatory validity and stability.

Tech Stack: R · tidyverse · ggplot2 · stats · factoextra
